## Do Student Loans Impact Undergraduate Participation in STEM Fields?
*Undergraduate Economics Research Paper — Kathleen Ulanday, College of William and Mary*

This paper examines whether student loans influence undergraduate participation 
in STEM fields using data from the IPUMS Higher Ed and National Surveys of 
Recent College Graduates (1995, 1997). Employing regression models and 
diagnostic tests, the study finds that student loans have a statistically 
insignificant effect on STEM participation. Instead, factors like gender, 
citizenship status, and full-time enrollment status emerge as more prominent 
determinants.
