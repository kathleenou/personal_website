Do Student Loans Impact Undergraduate Participation in STEM Fields?#

Undergraduate Economics Research Paper — Kathleen Ulanday, College of William and Mary

This paper examines whether student loans influence undergraduate participation in STEM fields using data from the IPUMS Higher Ed and National Surveys of Recent College Graduates (1995, 1997). Employing regression models and diagnostic tests, the study finds that student loans have a statistically insignificant effect on STEM participation. Instead, factors like gender, citizenship status, and full-time enrollment status emerge as more prominent determinants.

Does Trade Impact International Student Mobility? Evidence from USMCA Partners, 2013 – 2023#

Undergraduate Economics Research Paper — Kathleen Ulanday, College of William and Mary

This paper examines whether increased trade integration affects international student enrollment in the U.S. Using panel data from 2013–2023 across six countries, it analyzes trade volume and student flows via regression models and a difference-in-differences approach to assess USMCA’s impact on enrollment from Canada and Mexico. Results show trade volume positively associates with enrollment, but USMCA had no statistically significant effect. These findings suggest trade agreements play a limited role in shaping student mobility among economically advanced countries.
